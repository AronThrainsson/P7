Most common toxins in baby food

Common heavy metals


Arsenic: Often found in rice and rice-based products, it can affect brain development and increase cancer risk over time.

Lead: Detected in fruit juices and root vegetables, even low levels can harm cognitive development.

Cadmium: Present in spinach and carrots; chronic exposure can damage kidney function and bone health.

Mercury: Typically found in certain fish; while levels in baby food are lower, any exposure can impact brain development

Common pesticides


Chlorpyrifos: Linked to developmental issues and banned in several countries.

Glyphosate: Found in grains and oats; commonly used in agriculture.

Carbendazim: A fungicide in fruit products; poses potential fertility risks.

Permethrin: Affects the nervous system and is used on crops like peas and beans.

Atrazine: Used in corn production, it can seep into water sources and end up in food.

Common microplastics


Food packaging: Plastic containers and utensils can leach microplastics, especially when exposed to heat.

Water Contamination: Microplastics often contaminate drinking water used in baby food production.

Seafood: While less common, fish and shellfish may contain microplastics.

Fruits & Vegetables: These can absorb microplastics from contaminated soil and water.
