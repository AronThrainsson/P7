General advice

TIP 1


Understanding the ingredients.


  • Choose natural ingredients: Opt for baby food with minimal added sugars, artificial flavors, and preservatives. The simpler the ingredient list, the better, as fewer additives are often better for your baby’s developing digestive system.
  • Look out for allergens: Introduce new foods one at a time and wait a few days to observe for any allergic reactions, especially with common allergens like dairy, eggs, and nuts.
  • Avoid heavy metals: Some baby foods, particularly rice-based products, may contain higher levels of arsenic. Diversifying your baby’s diet with different grains like oats or barley can help minimize this risk.

TIP 2


Best Practices for Feeding Infants Over 6 Months.


  • Introduce solids at 6 months: Start with single-ingredient purees such as fruits (like apples or pears), vegetables (like carrots or sweet potatoes), and cereals.
  • Gradually increase texture: As your baby becomes accustomed to solids, slowly move from purees to thicker textures and small, soft chunks to encourage chewing.
  • Breast milk or formula first: Continue breastfeeding or formula feeding as the primary source of nutrition until your baby reaches one year.

TIP 3


What to look for in your baby’s food.


  • Organic and clean options: Organic baby foods are less likely to contain harmful chemicals, such as pesticides, making them a safer choice.
  • No added sugars or salt: Avoid baby foods with added sugar or salt, as these can overwhelm babies' undeveloped taste buds and kidneys.
  • Certifications and safety standards: Look for baby food brands that meet safety standards to ensure the products are free from harmful contaminants like lead, cadmium, and mercury.
