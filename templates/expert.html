What do the experts say?

Research Findings


Heavy metals, pesticides, and microplastics are major environmental hazards. They are found in food, water, and air, and can cause serious health problems.

The American Academy of Pediatrics has emphasized the importance of reducing these risks in infant diets, as heavy metals like lead, arsenic, and mercury can significantly impair brain development in young children.

Recent studies also indicate that microplastics, which have infiltrated even remote environments, may pose risks to human health by accumulating in tissues and potentially affecting organ function.

Pediatricians


Several pediatricians and experts emphasize the importance of reducing toxic chemical exposure in baby food, particularly heavy metals.

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) highlights the potential dangers of contaminants like lead, arsenic, and mercury, which can harm brain development and contribute to learning and behavior problems.

These metals can be found in both commercial and homemade baby foods, with rice-based products being especially prone to contamination​

Nutritionists


When it comes to baby nutrition and the presence of toxic chemicals in food, nutrition experts agree that while heavy metals like lead, cadmium, and arsenic can be a concern, the levels typically found in baby foods are low and unlikely to pose a significant risk when consumed in moderation.

For instance, Michelle Milgrim, a registered dietitian, emphasizes the importance of a varied diet to help mitigate exposure to heavy metals, recommending different grains like oats and quinoa instead of rice-based cereals, which are often higher in arsenic​.
